About Artropods
Juliia Pavliuk — Producer / Director
Dmytro Nedria — Cinematographer / Photographer / Director
Based between Barcelona & London. Operating Worldwide.

We're a creative duo running an independent production company between Barcelona and London, developing our own projects while also producing and servicing work for others.

Together, we build projects that live between cultures, formats, and worlds — mixing cinematic storytelling, strong visual language, fashion sensibility, documentary instinct, commercial precision and hybrid image-making approaches.

We don't really believe in creative boxes.
Or normal production limits.

We love mixing international crews, strange references, beautiful accidents, cinematic thinking, internet culture, and real human stories into something that feels alive.

Sometimes it's a film crew in the middle of nowhere.
Sometimes it's a two-person run-and-gun setup.
Sometimes it's a full commercial machine held together by coffee, taste, and production magic.

We work fast.
We care too much.
We only make things we actually believe in.
Operating worldwide.

2026
Hybrid Approach

Every person on screen was filmed for real. Faces, bodies, performances — all practical.

Actors were shot in the studio, while the world around them was expanded through AI using imagery built from our own archive captured on Madeira island.

Nothing here was generated from thin air. Every image started with something physical, photographed, observed, or captured by us first.

We used AI the same way filmmaking has always used tools: to extend reality, build impossible worlds, and push images further without compromising on human presence or cinematic language.

The grounding is always real.
The fantasy is built on top.

About the Film

A glimpse into the Healing Base Camp, a recovery space built by Mountain Seed Foundation for families affected by Russia's ongoing war against Ukraine.

Shot on-site in western Ukraine by a Ukrainian crew, the film follows a healing base camp built around mountains, movement, and human connection as a temporary space where families could breathe, recover, and feel safe again.
